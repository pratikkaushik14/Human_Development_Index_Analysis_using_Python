Introduction¶

The chosen subject matter of this project revolves around the analysis of Human Development Index (HDI) and its relationship with various societal factors. The HDI is a summary measure of human development that was introduced in 1990 by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) [1]. The UNDP evaluates and ranks countries based on three fundamental dimensions of human development: longevity, education and standard of living. Longevity is measured by life expectancy at birth, reflecting the overall health and longevity of a nation's population. The education dimension is measured through two metrics: expected years of schooling for children entering schools and the mean years of schooling for the adult population. The standard of living is measured using the Gross National Income (GNI) per capita. The purpose of this project is to uncover insights into how different aspects of human development interact with one another and to understand how countries' development is affected by a combination of factors, including gender inequality and environmental pressures. The motivation of this project is to understand changing trends in HDI, investigate gender disparities and their impact on development, and assess the environmental pressures affecting human development. Through this exploration, we aim to generate valuable insights that can inform policymakers, researchers, and society at large.

#Computing the HDI change for each decade and grouping by continents

#Use AVG function to compute the AVG HDI for the years 1990,2000,2010 and 2020
avg_hdi_change_query="""
SELECT 
    c.Continents,
    AVG(h.`HDI 1990`) as Avg_HDI_1990,
    AVG(h.`HDI 2000`) as Avg_HDI_2000,
    AVG(h.`HDI 2010`) as Avg_HDI_2010,
    AVG(h.`HDI 2020`) as Avg_HDI_2020,
    ((AVG(h.`HDI 2020`) - AVG(h.`HDI 1990`)) / AVG(h.`HDI 1990`)) * 100 as Avg_HDI_Percentage_Change_1990_to_2020
FROM 
    hdi_table2 h
INNER JOIN 
    country_continents c ON h.Country = c.Country
GROUP BY 
    c.Continents
ORDER BY 
    Avg_HDI_Percentage_Change_1990_to_2020 DESC;
"""

Guiding Question : 3¶

Does a country's overall development correlate with gender equality, and how does the gender gap potentially influence the per capita income of both men and women?¶

To address this, it's crucial to understand how each term is measured. A nation's overall development is often gauged using the Human Development Index (HDI), while gender inequality is assessed through the Gender Inequality Index (GII). Additionally, the per capita income of both genders contributes to the calculation of the Gender Development Index (GDI).

What is Human Development Index(HDI)?¶

(HDI) is a summary measure of average achievement in key dimensions of human development: a long and healthy life, being knowledgeable and having a decent standard of living This value is expressed between 0 and 1, with a higher HDI indicating greater human development.

What is Gender Inequality Index(GII)?¶

GII is a composite metric of gender inequality using three dimensions: reproductive health, empowerment and the labour market It ranges from 0 (indicating equality between genders) to 1 (representing substantial inequality).

What is Gender Developement Index(GDI)?¶

The GDI measures differences in male and female achievements in three basic dimensions of human development: health, education and command over economic resources. Gender disaggregated data is used in each dimension. The health dimension is captured by life expectancy at birth, female and male. Education is measured using two indicators— female and male expected years of schooling for children and female and male mean years of schooling for adults ages 25 and older.Command over economic resources is measured by female and male estimated earned income.

Importance:¶

HDI, or Human Development Index, doesn't factor in gender inequality when calculating a country's development, which is a drawback. This insight helps us grasp how developed countries fare in terms of gender inequality, offering a more detailed analysis beyond general development indicators and addressing specific gender-related challenges.

In the second part, looking into how the gender gap might affect the income of both men and women gives us insights into economic trends. Understanding the economic impacts of gender inequality is crucial for making informed policies and developing strategies to reduce economic disparities between genders.

WITH Mean AS(
SELECT 
        gender_inequality_index_value AS x,

        AVG(gender_inequality_index_value) OVER() AS mean_x,

JOIN
        hdi_table5
ON
        hdi_table1.country = hdi_table5.Country
),

-- Calculate Covariance and Denominator
Correlation AS(
SELECT
        AVG((x - mean_x)*(y- mean_y)) AS Covariance,
        STDDEV(x)*STDDEV(y) AS Denominator
FROM 
        Mean
)

-- Calculate correlation coefficient

FROM
